WIKIPEDIA

Wikipedia is a free, multilingual online encyclopedia written and maintained by a community of volunteer contributors through a model of open collaboration, using a wiki-based editing system. It is the largest and most popular general reference work on the internet, with more than 40 million articles in more than 250 different languages. Wikipedia was launched on January 15, 2001, by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger, and is currently owned by the non-profit Wikimedia Foundation. The name "Wikipedia" is a combination of the words "wiki" (a technology for creating collaborative websites) and "encyclopedia." Wikipedia's content is contributed by volunteers from all around the world, who write and edit articles on various topics. The site has a system of checks and balances in place to ensure the reliability and accuracy of its content, including a peer review process and a team of administrators who monitor and enforce the site's rules and guidelines. As an online encyclopedia, Wikipedia provides an easily accessible and free source of information on a wide range of subjects. However, it is important to note that it should not be considered the sole source of information on any given topic, and its accuracy may vary depending on the article.

    • PROJECT GUTENBERG

    Project Gutenberg (PG) is a volunteer-led initiative that aims to digitize and archive cultural works, focusing primarily on literature. The project collects public domain books and makes them available for free download, allowing people to access and enjoy these works regardless of their location or financial situation. Project Gutenberg was founded in 1971 by American writer and computer programmer Michael S. Hart. It is one of the oldest digital libraries, and as of April 2021, it has over 60,000 items in its collection. These items include books, as well as other texts like speeches, essays, and poetry. The books in the Project Gutenberg collection are typically available in several formats, including plain text, HTML, EPUB, and Kindle. Users can browse the library by author, title, language, or category, or they can search for specific books using keywords. In addition to the main Project Gutenberg site, there are several affiliated sites that focus on different languages or genres, such as Project Gutenberg Australia, Project Gutenberg Canada, and Project Gutenberg Self-Publishing Press. As a valuable resource for readers, researchers, and educators, Project Gutenberg continues to play an important role in preserving and promoting access to literature and other written works.

    • UNIVERSITY OF NAIROBI'S M-TIHANI website

    The University of Nairobi's M-TIHANI website is an online platform that provides educational resources and services to the university's students. M-TIHANI stands for Mobile Technology for Improved Higher Access and New Initiatives. The platform is designed to enhance the learning experience and improve access to educational resources for students. Some of the key features and services available on the M-TIHANI website include: Online course materials: Students can access course materials such as lecture notes, presentations, and additional reading resources for various subjects. Online assessments and quizzes: Instructors can create and administer quizzes, tests, and exams through the platform, allowing students to take assessments remotely and receive immediate feedback. Discussion forums: M-TIHANI provides a space for students to engage in discussions, ask questions, and collaborate with their peers and instructors. Grades and performance tracking: Students can view their grades and track their academic progress through the platform. Announcements and notifications: Instructors can share important updates, reminders, and announcements with their students through the platform. The University of Nairobi's M-TIHANI website is an essential tool for students, providing easy access to educational resources and fostering interaction between students and instructors.

    • MIT OpenCourseWare

    MIT OpenCourseWare (OCW) is an initiative by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) that provides free and open online access to the university's course materials, including syllabi, lecture notes, assignments, exams, and video lectures. Launched in 2002, OCW aims to make high-quality educational resources available to anyone in the world with an internet connection. The materials available on MIT OpenCourseWare cover a wide range of subjects, such as engineering, science, mathematics, humanities, arts, social sciences, and business. By offering these resources for free, MIT seeks to promote lifelong learning and advance education on a global scale. Key features and offerings of MIT OpenCourseWare include: Comprehensive course materials: OCW offers complete course materials for over 2,500 MIT courses, providing a valuable resource for self-learners and educators alike. Video lectures: Many courses on OCW include video recordings of MIT lectures, giving users an immersive classroom-like experience. Instructor insights: Some courses feature additional instructor resources, such as teaching notes and tips for effective learning. Translations: To make the content more accessible, some courses and materials are available in languages other than English. Self-paced learning: Users can learn at their own pace, without registration or course credits, making education more flexible and accessible. MIT OpenCourseWare is a testament to the power of open education and has inspired similar initiatives at other universities around the world, providing valuable resources for millions of learners and educators.
